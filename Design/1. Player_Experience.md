# Solitaire Universe

# Player Experience

Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines the emotional journey of the player.

It does not describe features.

It describes what the player should feel.

Every feature, animation, sound, and interaction should support these emotions.

If an implementation creates a different feeling,

the implementation should change.

---

# Design Philosophy

Players rarely remember every feature.

Players always remember how a game made them feel.

The purpose of Solitaire Universe is not only to provide excellent Solitaire gameplay,

but to create a place players genuinely want to return to.

The emotional experience is therefore one of the project's highest priorities.

---

# Emotional Journey

## 01 — First Launch

### Player should feel

Curiosity.

Wonder.

Quiet anticipation.

The player should immediately sense that this is not a traditional Solitaire game.

Nothing should feel rushed.

The Universe already exists before the player arrives.

---

## 02 — The Intro

### Player should feel

The Universe is alive.

Something has become unstable.

The player is not watching a trailer.

The player is witnessing a moment inside the Universe.

The logo should feel earned,

not introduced.

---

## 03 — Entering the Universe Hub

### Player should feel

Peace.

Safety.

Belonging.

The Hub should feel familiar,

like returning to a place that quietly waited for them.

---

## 04 — Choosing a World

### Player should feel

Purpose.

Every World matters.

Every choice contributes to restoring balance.

The player is choosing where to help,

not simply choosing a game mode.

---

## 05 — Beginning a Solitaire Game

### Player should feel

Focus.

Calm.

Confidence.

The transition from Hub to gameplay should remove distractions,

allowing complete attention on the cards.

---

## 06 — Completing a Run

### Player should feel

Relief.

Progress.

Contribution.

The completion should feel meaningful,

not explosive.

The player restored something larger than the current match.

---

## 07 — Completing a Chapter

### Player should feel

Growth.

Responsibility.

Visible change should occur within the Universe.

Players should immediately understand

their actions had lasting impact.

---

## 08 — Completing the First Restoration

### Player should feel

Hope.

Fulfillment.

Not finality.

The player should understand

their journey has changed,

not ended.

---

## 09 — Returning Tomorrow

### Player should feel

The Universe remembered them.

Today's Anomalies are new.

The Universe continued while they were away.

The player is welcomed back,

never pressured.

---

# Emotional Curve

| Stage | Emotion |
| :---- | :---- |
| First Launch | Curiosity |
| Intro | Wonder |
| Hub | Belonging |
| World Selection | Purpose |
| Gameplay | Focus |
| Completion | Satisfaction |
| Restoration | Hope |

---

# Things Players Should Never Feel

The player should never feel lost.

The player should never feel punished for taking a break.

The player should never feel forced to log in.

The player should never feel they are completing chores.

The player should never feel the Universe exists only while the game is open.

---

# Design Validation

Whenever a new feature is proposed,

ask the following questions.

Does it strengthen the emotional journey?

Does it strengthen immersion?

Does it reinforce the living Universe?

Would players still understand its purpose without reading text?

If the answer is no,

the design should be reconsidered.

---

# One Sentence

Players should remember how Solitaire Universe made them feel,

long after they forget the matches they played.
