# Solitaire Universe

# Audio Language

Version 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines how the Universe sounds.

Audio should create presence,

not distraction.

---

# Philosophy

Silence is part of the soundtrack.

The Universe does not constantly speak.

Players should notice sound because it matters.

---

# Core Principles

## Silence

Silence creates scale.

Silence creates attention.

Silence allows important moments to breathe.

---

## Atmosphere

Ambient sound should suggest an ancient Universe.

Soft energy.

Distant resonance.

Subtle movement.

Never obvious.

---

## Interaction

Every interaction should feel deliberate.

Buttons.

Cards.

Transitions.

Each sound should reinforce precision.

---

## Restoration

Progress deserves acknowledgement.

Not celebration.

When a World grows brighter,

the sound should suggest harmony,

not victory.

---

## Anomalies

Anomalies should feel unfamiliar.

Not evil.

Not hostile.

Simply wrong.

Their sound should contrast with the calm Universe.

---

# Music

Music supports emotion.

It should never dominate attention.

Players should feel immersed,

not directed.

---

# Dynamic Audio

The Universe should become subtly richer

as restoration progresses.

The player may not consciously notice,

but should feel the difference.

---

# Intro Principle

Music should emerge naturally,

like discovering an ancient place.

The logo should arrive together with the emotional climax,

not with the loudest sound.

---

# Design Validation

Ask:

Does this sound strengthen immersion?

Would silence be better?

Is the sound communicating emotion or simply providing feedback?

---

# One Sentence

The Universe speaks most clearly through its silence.
