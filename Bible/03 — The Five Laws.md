# The Solitaire Universe Bible

# 03 — The Five Orders

Version 0.1 (Draft)

---

# Purpose

This chapter defines the Five Orders.

They are not game modes.

They are the fundamental truths that allow the Universe to exist.

---

# The Five Orders

Each World represents one Order.

Each Order expresses a different way that order can emerge from cards.

The Orders are equal.

None is more important than another.

The Universe requires all five.

---

# Orders, Not Variants

Klondike.

Spider.

FreeCell.

Pyramid.

TriPeaks.

To the player they are familiar Solitaire games.

Within the Universe they are living Orders.

Gameplay and world-building are therefore inseparable.  
Each Order is expressed through a Solitaire Variant. The Variant is not the Order itself. It is the way the Keeper experiences and restores it.

---

# Balance

A single weakened Order affects the entire Universe.

The Orders exist independently,

but they survive together.

The restoration of one Order benefits all.

The loss of one Order weakens all.

---

# Growth

Every completed Chapter strengthens its Order.

As Orders recover,

the Universe visibly changes.

Progress is shared,

not isolated.

---

# Design Principles

Every Order must feel:

Distinct.

Recognizable.

Timeless.

Elegant.

Meaningful.

The goal is not variety.

The goal is harmony.

No Order is greater than another.

---

# One Sentence

The Five Orders are five different truths that together preserve balance.
